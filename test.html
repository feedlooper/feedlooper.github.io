About FeedLoop

Building the local circular protein system.

FeedLoop exists to close the gap between organic waste generation and the growing demand for sustainable agricultural inputs. We are developing a regional bioconversion platform that helps turn underused food system by-products into commercially useful products.

1

Local waste streams

We focus on pre-consumer organic by-products from food and agriculture partners in Western Canada.

2

Controlled production

Organic material is processed through a modular larvae-based system designed for scalable deployment.

3

Regional outputs

Products are directed back into feed, fertilizer, and agriculture markets to keep value circulating locally.

Nature’s conversion engine, built for scale.

Black Soldier Fly larvae are efficient biological converters. FeedLoop pairs that biology with modular infrastructure to create a compact pathway from organic by-products to high-value outputs.

BIOLOGY

Black Soldier Fly larvae

BSF larvae naturally convert organic material into nutrient-rich biomass, making them a powerful platform for circular food systems.

SYSTEM

Modular bioconversion

Our production approach is designed around scalable, controlled units that can grow with regional supply and demand.

OPERATION

From waste handling to product flow

The system connects intake, preparation, conversion, drying, separation, and product distribution into one practical operating model.

Three outputs. One circular system.

FeedLoop is built as a product platform — converting organic by-products into ingredients and soil inputs with real commercial use.

◒

Insect protein meal

A sustainable protein ingredient for animal feed applications, with potential use across poultry, aquaculture, pet food, and feed formulation markets.

◍

Insect oil

A functional fat source that can support feed and pet nutrition applications while creating additional value from the larvae biomass.

✦

Frass fertilizer

An organic soil input that returns nutrients and biological value to growers, helping close the loop between food production and soil health.
