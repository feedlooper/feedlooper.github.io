The Loop

A simple system with multiple product pathways.

Organic material enters the system, biology converts it, and products return to feed and soil markets.

01

Organic by-products

Pre-consumer food and agricultural streams from processors, farms, packers, and distributors.

02

Larvae conversion

Black Soldier Fly larvae convert organic material into nutrient-dense biomass.

03

Product processing

Larvae biomass and frass are separated, dried, and prepared as usable outputs.

04

Back to market

Protein, oil, and fertilizer move into feed and agriculture supply chains.

About

Built to make circularity practical.

FeedLoop is developing a regional bioconversion platform for organic by-products. The goal is to turn underused food system streams into commercially useful products for feed, fertilizer, and agriculture.

Technology

Biology inside a controlled production system.

Black Soldier Fly larvae are the conversion engine. FeedLoop builds the intake, production, processing, and market pathways around them.

System

From intake to output.

  • Organic stream intake and preparation
  • Larvae rearing and bioconversion
  • Drying, separation, and product finishing
  • Distribution into feed and fertilizer markets
